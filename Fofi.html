Fofi Constantinidou




Fofi Constantinidou, PhD, CCC-SLP, is Professor of Language Disorders and Clinical Neuropsychology and former Chair of Psychology at the University of Cyprus. She received her doctoral degree in speech-language pathology with a focus on neurogenic language and cognitive disorders from the University of Cincinnati in 1995. She then worked as a Professor in the Speech Pathology and Audiology department at Miami University. In 2006, Dr. Constantinidou joined the Department of Psychology at the University of Cyprus where she directs the Center for Applied Neuroscience, the premier research center for the behavioral sciences in Cyprus (http://www.cancyprus.org/) . Her research, teaching, and clinical expertise center on acquired adult neurological disorders and their effects on language, learning, memory, executive systems, and categorization. She has published extensively in this area and has received several grants for her work, including funding for clinical trials research from the National Institutes of Health (USA), the Cyprus Research Promotion Foundation, and the European Union. Constantinidou is a member of the American Speech Language Pathologist Association (ASHA), the Cypriot Association of Registered Speech-Language Pathologists, the International Neuropsychological Society, and the International Association of Logopaedics and Phoniatrics. She has served as the ASHA Chair of the Joint Committee on Interprofessional Relations between the American Speech-Language Hearing Association and the American Psychological Association (APA-Division 40, Clinical Neuropsychology). Constantinidou is Chair of the International Networking Group of the American Congress of Rehabilitation Medicine. She participates on many local and international professional and scientific committees and international scientific review boards.
